FAQ

Common Questions from Our Clients

Below are answers to the questions we hear most often from clients when starting a software project — covering timelines, pricing, scope changes, security, post-launch support, and communication.

Timelines depend on scope and complexity. Small projects (a single feature or simple site) typically take 4–8 weeks; medium projects (multiple integrations and custom UI) 3–6 months; larger platforms can take 6+ months. We always begin with a discovery phase to define requirements and deliver a milestone-based plan and realistic schedule.
We offer fixed-price contracts for well-defined scopes, and time-and-materials (T&M) for projects expected to evolve. For long-term partnerships we also provide retainer and dedicated-team options. You’ll receive a clear estimate after discovery and regular budget updates during delivery.
We follow an Agile approach: after discovery we maintain a prioritized backlog and deliver in short iterations. If requirements change, we assess impact, reprioritize, and provide an estimate for additional work before proceeding — keeping you in control of scope and budget.
Security is integrated into our workflow: threat modeling, secure-by-design patterns, dependency and SAST scanning, code reviews, and secure deployment practices. We can also assist with compliance requirements (GDPR, HIPAA, etc.) and provide recommendations for hosting and data protection.
Yes. We offer support plans ranging from ad-hoc fixes to SLA-backed maintenance, monitoring, and regular updates. We can also run health checks, urgent security patches, and roadmap planning to keep your product secure and evolving.
We assign a dedicated project manager and use tools like Jira, Trello, or Asana for task tracking. Communication includes weekly demos, sprint reviews, and scheduled status calls. You’ll have direct access to the team for questions and real-time updates.
